# Product

## Register

product

## Users
A single collector — the tool's author — who actively hunts vintage Timex watches across eBay, Etsy, and Chrono24. Uses the app as a focused daily check to catch good deals before they sell. Context: personal, low-noise, action-oriented.

## Product Purpose
Aggregate, filter, and score vintage Timex listings from multiple marketplaces using AI. Surface purchase-ready candidates ranked by taste, value, and freshness — so the collector can act quickly on genuinely good finds without wading through junk.

## Brand Personality
Warm · Curated · Personal. Feels like a collector's private cabinet: deliberate, quiet confidence. Not a marketplace, not a dashboard — a personal instrument for someone who takes the hobby seriously.

## Anti-references
- eBay / Craigslist: cluttered, dense, utilitarian marketplace UI
- Generic SaaS dashboard: card grids, blue primary buttons, sidebar nav with icons, metric tiles
- Watch forum density: table-heavy layouts, small text everywhere, no hierarchy

## Visual register
Minimal editorial serif. Newsreader for display type, Geist for UI chrome. Palette is near-black on near-white — no accent color; black (#111110) is the interactive color throughout. Warmth is carried by the Newsreader letterforms, watch photography, and subtle surface differentiation — not tinted backgrounds or gold. The aesthetic reads like a focused collector's publication: Hodinkee without the splash.

## Design Principles
1. **Warmth through materials, not background tint** — warmth lives in accent color, watch imagery, and selected typographic moments; the body bg is not responsible for communicating warmth.
2. **Collector's confidence** — the app should feel decisive and low-noise; every element has a reason to exist; nothing decorative.
3. **Surface the signal** — the score and the reason are the product; everything else is chrome that should recede.
4. **Personal, not public** — single-user tool; intimacy over scalability; no enterprise patterns, no onboarding scaffolding.
5. **Action-ready information** — a good listing should be act-on-able in one glance; the primary path is: see it, understand it, decide.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA minimum contrast on all text. No specific known user needs beyond the single-user context.
